Resolving Issues

Resolving issues means helping users fix problems they face with their computers, software, or network. This is often done through an IT Help Desk. Some examples are:

  • Software problems: Fixing errors, crashes, or bugs in programs.
  • Hardware problems: Repairing or replacing broken devices.
  • Network problems: Helping users connect to Wi-Fi or access shared drives.
  • Password resets: Helping users recover or change their passwords.
  • Virus removal: Scanning and removing harmful software from computers.

IT Help Desk staff listen to user problems, provide clear instructions, and sometimes visit users to solve bigger issues.
